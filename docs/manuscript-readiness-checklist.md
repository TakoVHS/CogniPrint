# Manuscript Readiness Checklist

This checklist records what must be true before CogniPrint should be treated as ready for a stronger manuscript submission pass.

## Current conclusion

CogniPrint is not yet ready for formal submission as a completed empirical study.

It does support a follow-up manuscript as a:

> working empirical evidence package supporting a follow-up manuscript

## Methods readiness

Methods are closer to readiness when:

- the campaign workflow is explained without relying on local-only context;
- provenance boundaries are explicit;
- benchmark and validation layers are described consistently with the current repository state;
- perturbation-family design is understandable to a reviewer;
- manuscript-facing method sections match the public evidence files.

Current status:

- partially ready for a follow-up draft;
- still needs tightening around benchmark growth and validation `v1.2`.

## Results readiness

Results are closer to readiness when:

- the current empirical base is described consistently as `5` campaigns and `41` comparison rows;
- benchmark subset counts are reported consistently as `6` baselines and `36` variants;
- validation `v1.1` outputs are described as descriptive and benchmark-linked;
- no section implies stronger generalization than the benchmark supports.

Current status:

- strong enough for a follow-up draft;
- not strong enough for completed-study framing.

## Limitations readiness

Limitations are closer to readiness when:

- corpus size limits are explicit;
- benchmark size limits are explicit;
- descriptive versus inferential boundaries are explicit;
- reviewers can see that the hosted layer, if present, does not change the scientific claim boundary.

Current status:

- mostly ready;
- still needs benchmark-growth and validation-forward wording kept visible.

## Evidence-table readiness

Evidence tables are closer to readiness when:

- campaign counts are stable and consistent across docs;
- benchmark coverage is summarized clearly;
- validation bridge summaries are available;
- provenance summaries are linked into the manuscript-facing package.

Current status:

- ready for reviewer use;
- not yet final for a stronger empirical appendix.

## Missing pieces

The following are still missing before a stronger manuscript submission pass:

- external review feedback integrated into the manuscript layer;
- expanded public benchmark coverage;
- statistical validation `v1.2` beyond the current descriptive layer;
- stronger benchmark-backed support for broader comparative claims.

## Decision rule

Before any formal submission push, the project should satisfy all of the following:

1. external review completed on the frozen reviewer release or its successor;
2. benchmark subset expanded beyond the current small release;
3. validation plan advanced beyond descriptive `v1.1` where justified;
4. claims and non-claims rechecked against the expanded evidence layer.

## Guardrail

Until those conditions are met, the manuscript should be treated as:

- a follow-up draft;
- a reviewable empirical package;
- not yet a formal completed-study submission.
