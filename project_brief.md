# CogniPrint – Project Brief

## Mission

CogniPrint is a mathematical research programme focused on the construction and comparison of compact statistical profiles of text, termed *cognitive fingerprints*. The goal is to develop reproducible methodologies for analysing empirical regularities in written language and to provide a foundation for scholarly publication, citation, and long-term research visibility.

## Research Questions

- How can we build compact feature vectors that capture stable statistical properties of a text?
- What geometric structures emerge in the space of cognitive fingerprints?
- Under what conditions do profiles from different sources exhibit measurable similarity or divergence?
- How sensitive are these profiles to controlled perturbations of the input text?

## Methodology Overview

1. **Feature Extraction**  
   Compute a set of distributional and structural metrics from the input text.

2. **Fingerprint Construction**  
   Combine the extracted features into a fixed-length vector representation — the cognitive fingerprint.

3. **Profile Comparison**  
   Define similarity and distance functions in the fingerprint space to enable quantitative comparison of texts.

4. **Empirical Analysis**  
   Perform controlled experiments to study the stability, separability, and geometric properties of the fingerprint space.

## Scientific Framing

All public communication about CogniPrint must adhere to the following vocabulary:

Allowed terms include:
- cognitive fingerprint
- mathematical profile
- statistical signature
- profile similarity
- heuristic assessment
- empirical regularities

Avoid product-like, accusatory, or over-strong language such as source finality, automated source judgement, investigative finality, legal conclusions, guaranteed classification, or product detection claims.

## Citation and Visibility

CogniPrint maintains:
- `CITATION.cff` for academic citation
- `.zenodo.json` for automatic DOI assignment on releases
- versioned research notes and mathematical documentation

The long-term objective is to establish CogniPrint as a recognised mathematical methodology.

## One-Sentence Positioning

**CogniPrint is a mathematical research framework for cognitive fingerprint analysis, profile comparison, and reproducible study of statistical regularities in text.**
